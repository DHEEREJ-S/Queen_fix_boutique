A-Line Lehenga

A-line lehenga is a popular style of lehenga, which is a traditional Indian skirt worn by women, especially during weddings, festivals, and other special occasions. The A-line lehenga gets its name from its silhouette, which resembles the letter "A" when spread out.

Half Saree Lehenga

A half saree lehenga, also known as a half saree or lehenga saree, is a modern fusion outfit that combines elements of both a traditional saree and a lehenga choli. It is a popular choice among women, especially for weddings, receptions, and other festive occasions.

Mesh Lehenga

A mesh lehenga is a type of lehenga choli, which is a traditional Indian outfit typically worn for weddings, festivals, and other special occasions. The term "mesh" refers to the material used in the construction of the lehenga, which is typically a sheer or translucent fabric with an open weave.

Multi Layers Lehenga

A multi-layered lehenga is a variation of the traditional Indian lehenga choli that incorporates multiple layers of fabric in the skirt portion. This style adds volume, richness, and a sense of grandeur to the outfit, making it a popular choice for weddings, receptions, and other formal occasions.

Sharara Cut Lehenga

A Sharara cut lehenga is a fusion style of Indian ethnic wear that combines elements of a traditional lehenga choli with the silhouette of a Sharara. Sharara is a traditional outfit consisting of loose, flared pants that are typically worn with a short kurti (tunic) and a dupatta (scarf). A Sharara cut lehenga maintains the flared silhouette of the Sharara pants while incorporating the style of a lehenga choli.

Short Lehenga

Short lehengas are a contemporary variation of the traditional Indian lehenga choli, characterized by a shorter length of the lehenga skirt. These skirts typically fall above the ankle or knee, offering a modern and chic alternative to the classic floor-length lehenga.
